Fourth Principle: Justice


The final principle is justice.

Justice requires health care providers to treat patients, and potentially the patients' caregivers, in a fair or just manner.

In certain situations, fair treatment will require equal treatment, but in other situations, a patient could be treated unequally but fairly.

Most commonly, consideration of justice takes priority in dilemmas about the allocation of limited health resources between patients.
